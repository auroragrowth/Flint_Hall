Welcome to Flint Hall

Where history meets charm

A rustic outdoor venue tucked into 17 acres of Suffolk countryside — built for the moments that matter, run by people who care that they go right.

Flint Hall is a working countryside venue, not a polished wedding factory. There’s a barn that takes a DJ at full tilt and an open-fronted Ranch where the bar lives. There’s a wood-fired pizza oven we’ve never lent to anyone, a meadow that holds the ceremony, and a fire pit that holds the conversation long after dinner’s gone.

For weddings, corporate retreats, proms and team building days, we offer the whole place — barn, ranch, meadow, padel court, accommodation field — with on-site catering, beds for up to twelve, and the kind of low-key hospitality that makes a long day feel short.

Heritage & home

A family home, first

Forty-five years, one family

Owned and lived in by the same family for over four decades — and a working cattle farm long before that.

The house and the big barn date back to the late 1700s and early 1800s. Look closely at the brickwork or up at the old beams and you’ll find the initials and dates of those who came before, etched in by hand. When the family first bought the place, they lived in caravans on site while it was made liveable. The original hand-pump that served the house is still here, still in its spot.

The seventeen acres are looked after, not manicured. The hedgerows are left wild on purpose, and the wild meadow is home to roe and muntjac deer, foxes, badgers and barn owls — usually heard before they’re seen.

Although the house and barns aren’t formally listed, the big barn is recognised as being of heritage interest. The Grade I listed church of Creeting St Peter is a short walk away, due north past the meadow. The whole site runs on solar with mains as backup.
